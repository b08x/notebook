In addition to a borderline obsession with optimizing systems and applications, I take great personal interest in experimenting with sound to alleviate aspects of the human condition we all experience. The potential I see in Calm as a long term reliable source for this type of information, is the ability to organize and translate widely scattered bits of this challenging data into an enjoyable user experience. While I have not demoed the app, I do appreciate the content and usability of the website. I was pretty happy to see a Stephen Fry expert I haven't heard before, so thank you.  

I believe once compression and subjective loudness issues are fully worked out and optimized, soundscapes with positive messages will enhance the life experience in many profound ways even beyond what we aim for today. As the scope of "mindfulness metadata" has yet to be fully realized, I would quite enjoy the opportunity to participate and assist in this endeavor where needed. The requirements of learning (internal tools) as well as advocating for users in distress, are core functions I incorporate in my daily life regardless, so I think this position could be a good fit.

My experience for past decade or so mainly revolves around Linux server configuration and support. I'm fairly proficient in Ruby, intimately familiar with Ansible, pretty ok with Docker and I always enjoy the feeling of a `git fetch && git pull` that doesn't result in a merge conflict. When prompted or asked, I do advocate the Unix software philosophy; building simple, short, clear, and modular functions. While I don't consider myself a "full-stack" engineer as much as systems engineer who enjoys solving problems with scripting, I do have understanding of design concepts and workflows that I hope would be of use in the position of Support Specialist if not in other areas. 

I'll leave the rest up to your imagination. Thank you for your time.

- Bob
